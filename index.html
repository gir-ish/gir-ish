SPEECH

Modality-Order Matters!
A Novel Hierarchical Feature Fusion Method for CoSAm: A Code- Switched Autism Corpus
journal

The research introduces a novel hierarchical feature fusion method for detecting Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in children through speech analysis. Utilizing a code-switched (English and Hindi) speech corpus named CoSAm, the method integrates acoustic, paralinguistic, and linguistic features using Transformer Encoders. Key findings:
Achieved 98.75% accuracy in ASD detection.
Provides insights into speech patterns of bilingual or multilingual children with ASD.

SPEECH

NeuRO: An Application for Code-Switched Autism Detection in Children





INTERSPEECH 24 Show & Tell Demonstrations

NeuRO detects Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in children using code-switched speech. It employs advanced speech recognition and paralinguistics, utilizing Whisper and TRILLsson models. Machine learning models analyze these features for high detection accuracy. The user-friendly web interface supports easy audio uploads and early ASD intervention. Combining linguistic and paralinguistic features enhances detection.

SPEECH

Multi-View feature Aggregation for Depression Detection from Short Segments of Speech



journal

The project introduces a method using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) to detect depression from brief speech segments. By combining multiple feature representations, such as XVECTOR, EMOTION, and TRILLSSON.The project demonstrates that feature aggregation significantly enhances depression detection accuracy across varying speech segment durations, underscoring the critical role of feature engineering in mental health diagnostics.
